[TOPIC: NAME CORRECTION (SPELLING ERROR)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Original CNIC
- Any ONE of: Matric Certificate / Educational Certificate / Pakistani Passport

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: NAME CHANGE AFTER MARRIAGE (FEMALE)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NEW 2025 RULE: Married women now have the CHOICE to display either their father's name
OR their husband's name on their CNIC.
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Original CNIC
- Nikahnama (Marriage Certificate)
- Nikah registered with Union Council

PROCESS: Visit NRC > Request Marital Status/Name update > Provide biometrics > Submit documents > Pay Rs. 100 (non-printable field update).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: NAME CHANGE AFTER DIVORCE]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Original CNIC
- Divorce Certificate / Khula Certificate from court
- Updated Family Registration Certificate (FRC)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: DATE OF BIRTH (DOB) MODIFICATION]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED (any one of):
- Valid Pakistani passport with correct DOB
- Valid foreign passport with correct DOB
- Biometric Residence Permit (BRP) — for UK applicants
- Valid Visa / Resident Permit / Work Permit / PR Card
- Matriculation Certificate issued by Pakistani BISE (Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education)
- Service Book issued by Government of Pakistan
- Court Order issued by Pakistani Court of Law

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: PARENTAGE / FATHER-MOTHER NAME MODIFICATION]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Court Order from a Pakistani Court of Law (mandatory)
- Court order must be verified by NADRA or concerned Mission
- In overseas cases, High Commission verifies court order before processing

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: MARITAL STATUS CHANGE (SINGLE TO MARRIED)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Original CNIC
- Nikahnama / Marriage Certificate
- Marriage must be registered at Union Council BEFORE applying
FEE: Rs. 100 (non-printable field update; change reflects in NADRA database but not on printed card face)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: MARITAL STATUS CHANGE (MARRIED TO DIVORCED)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Original CNIC
- Divorce Certificate / Talaq Nama from court

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: MARITAL STATUS CHANGE (MARRIED TO WIDOWED)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Original CNIC
- Death Certificate of spouse

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: ADDRESS MODIFICATION]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED (any one of):
- Property documents in applicant's name
- Tenancy agreement + owner's affidavit
- Utility bill (electricity/gas/water)
- Blood relative's CNIC with same address

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: PHOTOGRAPH UPDATE]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Scanned/photo copy of existing CNIC
- Photograph taken at NRC (live capture at NADRA center mandatory for photo update)

NOTE: Renewal automatically updates the photograph on the card.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: RELIGION UPDATE]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DOCUMENTS REQUIRED:
- Affidavit
- Relevant religious certificate (if changing to a minority religion)
NOTE: Religion is recorded in NADRA's system but NOT printed on the card face.

[SECTION: NICOP MODIFICATION — REQUIRED DOCUMENTS BY FIELD]
================================================================================

Fields that can be modified on Smart NICOP/NICOP:
- Name
- Father and Mother Name Correction
- Marital Status
- Address
- Date of Birth
- Place of Birth
- Photograph
- Signature
- Country of Stay
- Country of Birth
- Mother Language
- Addition of Relative
- Qualification
- Contact Detail

MARITAL STATUS MODIFICATION:
- Marriage: Nikahnama / Marriage Certificate
- Divorce: Divorce Certificate
- Widow: Death certificate of spouse + Affidavit

DATE OF BIRTH:
- Valid Pakistani/Foreign passport OR BRP OR valid visa OR Matric Certificate OR Court Order

PARENTAGE NAME:
- Court Order from Pakistani Court of Law (verified by Mission)

IMPORTANT NOTE FOR OVERSEAS APPLICANTS:
- Foreign address can only appear in ONE address field (Present OR Permanent).
  The other field MUST be a Pakistan address.
- Fingerprints/signatures NOT required for children under 15 years.
- Attestation is MANDATORY for all Smart ID overseas applications.
- If no blood relative available: two Pakistani citizen witnesses with valid CNIC/NICOP
  (must not be parents, children, spouse, or siblings) required.

================================================================================
[SECTION: FRESH CNIC — WHO QUALIFIES WITHOUT BLOOD RELATIVES]
================================================================================

If a citizen does NOT have any blood relative with a CNIC/NICOP:
- Fingerprints of any Pakistani citizen holding CNIC/NICOP can be taken as witness.
- Case will be forwarded to Pakistan headquarters for further verification.
- This applies in overseas applications too.

Citizens without parents, siblings, or children (family-less cases):
- Cannot apply online; must visit NRC in person.
- NADRA requires additional legal verification.
- Gazetted Officer attestation can substitute blood relative biometrics.

[SECTION: WHO CAN ATTEST / VERIFY THE SMART ID VERIFIER FORM]
================================================================================

METHOD 1 — BIOMETRIC VERIFICATION (via Pak ID Mobile App):
- Father, Mother, Brother, Sister, Son (18+), Daughter (18+)
- Paternal/Maternal Grandfather/Grandmother, Spouse
NOTE: Verifier captures fingerprints + digital signature in the mobile app.

METHOD 2 — ATTESTATION BY AUTHORIZED PERSONS:
- Member of Senate, National/Provincial Assembly, Local Body, or Zakat Committee
- Federal/Provincial Government Officers in Basic Pay Scale 16 and above
- Commissioned Officers of armed forces and civil armed forces
- Officers of local bodies (equivalent to BPS-16)
- Officers of autonomous/semi-autonomous bodies
- Officers of Levies in BPS-16 and above
- Officers of Government Bank and Government Corporations
- Headmen (Lambardar) for persons living in their village
- Officers of NADRA in BPS/NPS/Contractual scale 16 and above
- Head of family (Grade 16+) can attest family members (NOT their own form)
- Political Agent (PA) or Assistant Political Agent (APA)

[TOPIC: NEW CNIC AND B-FORM RULES (June 2025)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SOURCE: Federal Cabinet approved amendments to NADRA NIC Rules, June 2025.

KEY CHANGES:
1. BIRTH REGISTRATION MANDATORY: Computerized Birth Registration Certificate from Union
   Council is now mandatory BEFORE issuance of CRC (B-Form). No birth registration = no B-Form.

2. CHILDREN'S BIOMETRIC REQUIREMENTS UPDATED:
   - Under 3 years: Exempt from biometrics and photographs (unless for passport).
   - 3 to 10 years: Photograph mandatory + iris scan (where available).
   - 10 to 18 years: Biometrics (fingerprints), photograph, AND iris scan are mandatory.
   Each child gets an individual CRC with a specified validity period.

3. B-FORM FOR PASSPORT: New (updated) B-Form is now a prerequisite for passport applications.
   Old B-Forms remain valid, but updated one is required for passports.

4. FRC LEGAL STATUS: Family Registration Certificate (FRC) has been granted legal status.
   Applicants must sign a declaration confirming accuracy of family information.
   FRC now includes full details of spouses in cases of multiple marriages.

5. WOMEN'S CHOICE ON CNIC: Married women can now choose to display either their
   FATHER'S name OR their HUSBAND'S name on their CNIC — at their preference.

6. FAMILY TYPES FORMALLY DEFINED:
   - Alpha: By birth (parents and siblings)
   - Beta: By marriage (spouse and children)
   - Gamma: By adoption (guardian and adopted family)

7. VOLUNTARY CORRECTION FACILITY: Citizens with incorrect identity records can proactively
   report to NADRA for legal protection and correction.

8. BIOMETRICS OFFICIALLY DEFINED: Biometrics is now formally defined in regulations as
   personal data derived from physical/physiological/behavioral characteristics (facial images,
   fingerprints) used for unique identification. All institutions (State Bank, FBR, PTA) must align.

9. CHIPLESS IDENTITY CARD INTRODUCED: Lower-cost identity card option:
   - No physical chip
   - Bilingual data (Urdu + English)
   - QR code for instant digital verification
   - Faster delivery, more affordable

[TOPIC: NADRA PAK ID MOBILE APP TRANSITION (January 2025)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- NADRA has migrated its primary service platform FROM the Pak ID website TO the Pak ID Mobile App.
- The Pak ID Mobile App (Android and iOS) is now the PRIMARY channel for all online services.
- Fresh/New CNIC STILL requires in-person NRC visit; all other categories available via app.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: PASSPORT BIOMETRICS VIA PAK ID APP (2025)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- DGIP (Directorate General of Immigration & Passports) now allows fingerprint verification
  for online passport applications via the NADRA PAKID Mobile App.
- This replaces the need for a manual fingerprints form at the fingerprint upload stage.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: DIGITAL IDENTITY (DIGITAL ID) — LAUNCHED 2025]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DESCRIPTION: NADRA now issues Digital Identity credentials through the PakID app.
- Secure, verifiable digital credentials stored in Pak ID Vault.
- Enables Single Sign-On (SSO) access to multiple government portals.
- QR codes on all documents for offline verification.
- Complies with GDPR, NIST 800-63, ISO/IEC 27001, OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect (OIDC).

ELIGIBILITY FOR DIGITAL ID:
- Must be at least 18 years old
- Must have a SIM registered on their CNIC
- Must complete biometric verification (photo + fingerprints via Pak ID app)

[SECTION: POC — RESUMPTION PROCESS (FORM Y) — DETAILED STEPS]
================================================================================

For ex-Pakistanis who wish to resume Pakistani nationality:

STEP 1: Applicant fills declaration in quadruplicate in Form Y at Pakistani Mission.
         Encloses original Renunciation Certificate + copies of CNIC/NICOP/POC.
STEP 2: Mission attests Form Y and attached documents, forwards to Dte Gen IMPASS.
STEP 3: Dte Gen IMPASS registers declaration, forwards to Mission AND NADRA.
STEP 4: Mission hands registration document to applicant.
STEP 5: Applicant provides registration document to NADRA to:
         - Surrender POC
         - Obtain NICOP (resuming Pakistani citizenship)

NOTE: Additional documents may be requested by the Case Officer to confirm Pakistani origin.

[SECTION: COMPLAINT MANAGEMENT — NCCMS]
================================================================================

NCCMS = NADRA Centralized Complaint Management System

WHEN TO USE NCCMS:
- Technical issues on Pak ID app or website
- Application errors (e.g., "already applied for application which is still in progress")
- Rejected application (if no email received from NADRA)
- Card received with errors
- Identity card not received after payment
- Unable to upload documents
- Citizen Number not verified

HOW TO CONTACT:
- Online portal: https://complaints.nadra.gov.pk/
- Phone (mobile users): 1777
- Phone (landline/international): +92 51 111 786 100
- For DUP overseas: dup.op@nadra.gov.pk

WHAT TO INCLUDE IN COMPLAINT:
- Tracking ID
- Citizen Number (13-digit ID number)
- Relevant screenshots/documents
- Bank name + card type (for payment issues)
- Country of application (for overseas applicants)

[TOPIC: APPLICATION REFERRAL TO NRC]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Your online application may be referred to a NADRA center if:
- You provided misleading information
- Your family details are incomplete
- Your record is not permanently cleared in NADRA database
- A family member's record is marked for verification

IMPORTANT: If referred to NRC, you can visit ANY NADRA center.
You DO NOT need to pay the fee again.
There is NO time limit for visiting NRC after referral.

[SECTION: PAK ID APP — COMMON ACCOUNT ISSUES AND SOLUTIONS]
================================================================================

ISSUE: "PakID account not verified, Citizen Number already associated with another account"
SOLUTION: You can only create ONE PakID account per identity card number.
Login to the existing account. Use "Forgot Password" to reset and login.

ISSUE: "Email already registered with another account"
SOLUTION: Each email address can only be used for one PakID account.
Use a different email address for your new account.

ISSUE: Fingerprint matching failure during account verification
SOLUTION: Try facial verification option. If that also fails, apply through a blood relative's
(father/mother/sibling/child 18+) PakID account. Or contact NCCMS to refresh fingerprint attempt count.

ISSUE: "Citizen Number not verified" error
SOLUTION: May indicate: DUP-marked record, cancelled record, or record marked for verification.
Visit NADRA center in person for processing.

ISSUE: "Error: You are not eligible to apply for Smart ID"
SOLUTION: Either your ID card expired more than one year ago, OR your record is marked for verification.
Visit any NADRA center.

ISSUE: Not receiving OTP/PIN in email during account creation
SOLUTION: Check Spam/Junk folder. The OTP is a 4-digit PIN.

ISSUE: Location permission error on Pak ID app
SOLUTION: Enable location services on mobile BEFORE opening the app.
Allow location permission when prompted. Ensure stable internet connection.

[SECTION: IMPORTANT NADRA RULES AND POLICIES]
================================================================================

CNIC VALIDITY: 10 years for adults (18 and above).

CNIC VALIDITY FOR MINORS: Issued for shorter durations; transitions to full CNIC at age 18.

CNIC IS REQUIRED FOR:
- Electoral processes (voting)
- Banking and financial transactions
- Issuance of passports
- Government and private employment
- Property transactions and inheritance
- Mobile SIM card issuance
- Government schemes, subsidies, welfare programs (BISP, Sehat Sahulat)
- Security clearance and general identification

LEGAL FACTS:
- Not applying for CNIC within 90 days of turning 18 is a criminal offence (NADRA Ordinance 2000, Section 30).
- Not registering a newborn child within 1 month of birth is also an offence.
- Providing fake or forged information to NADRA may lead to legal proceedings (Section 30, NADRA Ordinance 2000).
- Family members must cancel a deceased person's CNIC within 60 days of their death.

CNIC NUMBER STRUCTURE:
The 13-digit CNIC number is not random. Each digit group carries specific information about
the card holder's province, district, and registration sequence.

OVERSEAS APPLICANTS WITH CNIC/NICOP/POC: Must insert their existing ID number when applying
for NICOP/POC. Applying in New/Fresh category when you already have an ID number will result
in case closure; applicant must reapply and initiate a refund request.
