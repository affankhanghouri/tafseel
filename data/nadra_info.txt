[SECTION: FAMILY REGISTRATION CERTIFICATE (FRC)]

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FULL NAME   : Family Registration Certificate
SHORT NAME  : FRC
DESCRIPTION : An official document issued by the Authority which reflects, verifies and provides record of the family composition data of a registered person.
LEGAL BASIS : NADRA issues FRC in the format prescribed in Schedule XI of Rule 19 of the NADRA (NIC) Rules, 2002.
VALIDITY    : The FRC serves as valid proof of the family composition data held by the Authority on the date of its issuance.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - CATEGORIES]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
There are four categories under which FRC can be applied:

BY BIRTH    : Includes details of your parents and siblings.
BY MARRIAGE : Includes details of your spouse and children.
BY ADOPTION : Includes details of your guardian and adopted family members.
BY ALL      : Includes details of both alpha and beta family members.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - ELIGIBILITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- All family members should be registered with NADRA and have a valid 13-digit identification number.
- Unregistered deceased and alive (in certain scenarios) individuals can also be included.
- Particulars of unregistered family members are mentioned as per information provided by the applicant.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - FEES]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BY BIRTH       : Rs 1,000/-
BY MARRIAGE    : Rs 1,000/-
BY ADOPTION    : Rs 1,000/-
BY ALL         : Rs 2,000/-

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - HOW TO APPLY - METHOD 1: NADRA REGISTRATION CENTER (NRC)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
STEPS:
1. Visit the nearest NRC.
2. Obtain a token.
3. Biometric Verification.
4. Photographs of minors will be taken (if CRC is issued without Photo).
5. Data will be downloaded and reviewed.
6. Fee submission.
7. FRC will be printed and handed over.

PROCESSING TIME: FRC will be issued in real-time.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - HOW TO APPLY - METHOD 2: PAK ID MOBILE APP]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
APP NAVIGATION: Go Pak ID Mobile App > Family > FRC
DELIVERY: FRC will be delivered digitally in Mobile App (PDF) and appear in ID Vault upon completion of processing period.

STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE:
1. Log in to your Pak ID account or create a new account if you don't have one.
2. On "Home" page, go to "Family" and select "FRC".
3. Family Members Detail will appear.
4. Records highlighted in red require correction in relevant fields of data.
5. Select any of the following as per requirement: By Birth / By Marriage / By Adoption / By All.
6. Review and submit the application.
7. Fee submission.
8. FRC will be downloaded (PDF) and appeared in ID Vault upon completion of the processing period.

PROCESSING TIME: A PDF version will be available for download/print within 24 hours.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - REQUIREMENTS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REQUIRED: 13-digit ID number of the applicant (CRC / CNIC / NICOP / POC).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: FRC - IMPORTANT NOTES]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- If children are below 18 years of age and CRC is issued without photo, they must be physically present.
- Incomplete records will not appear in the FRC. Such records will be included after updating.
- If a POC holder does not exist in the family, he must apply and include other members as relatives.
- If a family member is deceased, his/her CNIC must be canceled by providing Death Certificate (issued by Union Council / Municipal Corporation / Cantonment) before processing of FRC.
- You can only correct English name spellings during processing of FRC; name change is not allowed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CRC - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FULL NAME    : Child Registration Certificate
SHORT NAME   : CRC
ALSO KNOWN AS: B-Form
DESCRIPTION  : An essential registration document for all children below 18 years of age.
LEGAL BASIS  : NADRA issues CRC in the format prescribed in Schedule VI of Rule 18 of the NADRA (NIC) Rules 2002.

LEGAL REQUIREMENT: As per Sub-section (1) of Section 9 of the NADRA Ordinance 2000, every parent or guardian of a citizen who has not attained the age of eighteen (18) years, is required to register such citizen within one month of birth.

LEGAL OFFENCE: As per Clause (a) of Sub-section (1) of Section 30 of NADRA Ordinance, 2000, any person who does not apply for registration required under this ordinance, commits an offence.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CRC - ELIGIBILITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Every Pakistani child under 18 years of age whose parent(s) possess a valid CNIC / NICOP is eligible to apply for a Child Registration Certificate (CRC).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CRC - DOCUMENT REQUIREMENTS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROOF OF BIRTH (INLAND BIRTHS)  : Computerized Birth Registration Certificate issued by the Union Council / Municipal Committee / Cantonment.
PROOF OF BIRTH (ABROAD BIRTHS)  : Detailed Birth Certificate or S-1 Form.
PARENT REQUIREMENT              : Parent(s) must hold Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC) or National Identity Card for Overseas Pakistanis (NICOP).

PHOTOGRAPH AND FINGERPRINT REQUIREMENTS BY AGE:
- Children up to 03 years      : Should accompany only if CRC is being processed for Passport issuance purpose.
- Children age 03 to 10 years  : Photograph is mandatory.
- Children age 10 to 18 years  : Photograph AND fingerprints are mandatory.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CRC - FEES]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EXECUTIVE : Rs 500 — Processing time: 01 day
NORMAL    : Rs 50  — Processing time: 07 days

2. Obtain a token.
3. Biometric Verification.
4. Photographs and/or fingerprints of children (where required).
5. Attestation by Spouse / Guardian / Gazetted officer.
6. Fee submission. (Executive Rs 500 – 01 days) & (Normal Rs 50 – 07 days).
7. CRC will be printed and handed over upon completion of the processing period against the specific category.

[TOPIC: CNIC - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FULL NAME    : Computerized National Identity Card
SHORT NAME   : CNIC / NIC
DESCRIPTION  : The primary identity document issued by NADRA. It serves as official proof of a citizen's identity, nationality and legal status in Pakistan.
ROLE         : The CNIC forms the foundation of Pakistan's identity management system and ensures secure, reliable and technology-based citizen registration.

CNIC IS REQUIRED FOR:
- Electoral processes
- Banking and financial transactions
- Issuance of passports
- Government and private employment
- Property transactions and inheritance matters
- Issuance of Mobile SIM cards
- Access to government schemes, subsidies, and welfare programs
- Security clearance and general identification

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CNIC - ELIGIBILITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
AGE REQUIREMENT: CNIC is issued to all Pakistani Citizens who have attained the age of 18 years.
LEGAL BASIS: As per Sub-section 1 of Section 9 of NADRA Ordinance, 2000, every such citizen in or out of Pakistan, shall get himself registered with NADRA within "One Month".
LEGAL OFFENCE: As per Clause (e) of Sub-section (1) of Section 30 of NADRA Ordinance, 2000, any person who does not apply for a National Identity Card within ninety days of his attaining the age of eighteen years, commits an offence.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CNIC - APPLICATION CATEGORIES]

MODIFICATION                        : Update details on your CNIC.
MODIFICATION IN NON-PRINTABLE FIELDS: Update details which are not printed on the card such as Marital status, Qualification, and Profession etc.
CANCELLATION                        : Request cancellation of an issued CNIC.

[TOPIC: CNIC - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: What is the "DUP" status?
A: Citizens who have obtained two (02) or more than two (02) identity card(s) / CRC(s) with different Identity Numbers are flagged as "DUP" in the NADRA database. Such individuals have to process for cancellation of additional identity card(s) / CRC(s) and retain only one (01) identity card / CRC issued with correct particulars.

Q: How can I get myself cleared from "DUP"?
A: Such individuals can visit any NADRA Registration Center (NRC) and apply for "DUP" clearance. The individual is required to submit a notarized affidavit as per prescribed format.
FEE: Rs. 1,000/- (for same particulars) and Rs. 10,000/- (for different particulars).
Upon submission, one of the card(s) / certificate(s) is retained while the other (duplicate) is surrendered automatically.
OVERSEAS PAKISTANIS: Must submit application and supporting documents to NADRA through email ID: dup.op@nadra.gov.pk

Q: I want to modify data on CNIC.
A: Please visit Registration Policy 6.0.1 in Downloads Section for documentary requirements against concerned Modification Type.

Q: I want to renew my identity card.
A: Scanned image of ID card (NICOP/CNIC) is required for renewal. Only Picture will be updated on the card.
NOTE: If you wish to modify any other field at the time of renewal, please apply in modification category.

Q: I want to modify data on my CNIC (Non-printable Field).
A: Please visit Registration Policy 6.0.1 in Downloads Section for documentary requirements in non-printable fields.

Q: My Identity Card (CNIC) is stolen / lost.
A: No document is required; card will be issued with existing data.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: POC - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FULL NAME   : Pakistan Origin Card
SHORT NAME  : POC
LEGAL BASIS : Rule 4 of the POC Rules, 2002 outlines the eligibility criteria.
DESCRIPTION : Issued to the eligible foreigners of Pakistani Origin to provide them secure and valid identity for their linkage with Pakistan.
ISSUED UNDER: Sub Rule (1), (4) & (5) of Rule 4 and Sub Rule (1) of Rule 5 of NADRA (POC) Rules, 2002.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: POC - BENEFITS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
POC holders can avail the following benefits till the validity of the card:

- May enter Pakistan without a visa from such port or place, by such route and in accordance with such conditions as are prescribed for citizens of Pakistan under section 13 of the Passport Act, 1974 (XX of 1974).
- Shall have permission to stay in Pakistan till the validity of the card.
- Shall, during their stay in Pakistan, be exempt as a Foreigner from reporting to, or registering with, any law enforcing or other governmental agency with which such report or registration in the capacity of a Foreigner is otherwise required under any law in force in Pakistan.
- May open bank accounts anywhere in Pakistan and transact business therein subject to fulfillment of the requirements of any other law for the time being in force.
- May, subject to any other law for the time being in force, enter into transaction of purchase and sale of movable or immovable property anywhere in Pakistan and deal with and dispose of such property.
- May use the card for establishing and proving his/her identity anywhere in Pakistan where a National Identity Card is required for the purpose.
- May obtain employment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: POC - ELIGIBILITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
POC is issued to individuals falling under the following categories:

1. Person who is not a citizen of Pakistan, but was citizen of Pakistan at any time in life (Rule 4(1) of the NADRA POC Rules, 2002).
2. An individual with any of Ex-Pakistani parents or grandparents (Either alive or deceased) (Rule 4(4) of the NADRA POC Rules, 2002). The individual is either minor or was never citizen of Pakistan.
3. The individual providing his/her domicile within the meaning of Succession Act, 1925 who or his/her any of the Parents or Grand Parents was born in territories now included in Pakistan (Rule 4(3) of the NADRA POC Rules, 2002).
4. The individual who acquired foreign nationality before commencement of Pakistan Citizenship Act 1951 (Rule 4(3) of the NADRA POC Rules, 2002).
5. Spouse of Pakistani citizen / Origin (Rule 4(5) of the NADRA POC Rules, 2002).
6. Dependent Child of Foreign spouse (Rule 5(1) of the NADRA POC Rules, 2002).

ALSO ELIGIBLE:
- You were a Pakistani national and have surrendered the nationality.
- You are a foreign national, married to a Pakistani citizen and your parents are of Pakistani origin or ever held Pakistani nationality.
- You are a foreign national and your parents or grandparents are or were Pakistani nationals.
- You are a foreign national and any of your relatives (brother, sister, uncle, aunt) are or were Pakistani nationals.

NOT ELIGIBLE (Foreign spouse):
- Is a citizen or national of India, Israel and Taiwan.
- Is a citizen or national of state or country not recognized by Pakistan.
- Is a citizen of any enemy country.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CANCELLATION CERTIFICATE - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DESCRIPTION: The cancellation of Identity documents is required due to the following reasons:
1. Cancellation of Identity documents due to death
2. Cancellation of ID documents due to surrender of Pakistani Nationality
3. Cancellation of POC due to Dual Nationality agreement or resumption of Pakistani Nationality
4. Cancellation of POC due to Divorce (For Foreign Spouse only)
5. DUP Cancellation

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CANCELLATION - TYPE 1: DUE TO DEATH]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEGAL BASIS: NADRA issues Cancellation Certificate when an individual's record is required to be formally cancelled due to his/her death under Sub-Section (1) of Section 17 of NADRA Ordinance, 2000.
RESPONSIBILITY: It is the responsibility of the concerned family member to process for cancellation of deceased person's record within sixty (60) days.

DOCUMENT REQUIREMENT:
- Computerized Death Certificate (Verified) issued by UC / Municipal Committee / Cantonment
  OR Foreign Death Certificate

FEE: No fee is charged for cancellation of deceased person's ID documents.

HOW TO APPLY:
- NADRA Registration Center (NRC): Obtain a token, complete biometric verification, review deceased information, submit application and receive Cancellation Certificate within 24 hours.
- Pak Identity Mobile App: Apply online and receive a digital Cancellation Certificate within 24 hours.
- APP NAVIGATION: Go Pak ID Mobile App > ID Documents > ID Cancellation

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CANCELLATION - TYPE 2: DUE TO SURRENDER OF PAKISTANI NATIONALITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEGAL BASIS: Pakistani citizens who have obtained the citizenship of those countries which do not have dual national arrangements with Pakistan, are required to surrender Pakistani Nationality under Section 14 of Pakistan Citizenship Act, 1951. Such individuals have to process for cancellation of their ID documents under Sub-Rule (2) of NADRA (NIC) Rules 2000.

DOCUMENT REQUIREMENT:
- Single National Country's Passport AND Undertaking "D" on Prescribed format
  OR Renunciation Certificate
  OR Dual National Country's Passport AND Renunciation Certificate (verified)

HOW TO APPLY:
- NADRA Registration Center (NRC): Obtain a token, complete biometric verification, review information, submit document, submit application, pay fee and receive Cancellation Certificate within two (02) days.
- Pak Identity Mobile App: Apply online and receive a digital Cancellation Certificate within two (02) days.
- APP NAVIGATION: Go Pak ID Mobile App > Surrender ID > Myself / My Blood Relatives

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CANCELLATION - TYPE 3: POC DUE TO DUAL NATIONALITY AGREEMENT OR RESUMPTION OF PAKISTANI NATIONALITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DESCRIPTION: Ex-Pakistanis who intend to resume the Pakistani nationality whether due to a new dual national agreement or the surrender of other Country's Nationality, are required to apply for the cancellation of POC.

DOCUMENT REQUIREMENT:
- Resumption letter / Citizenship certificate from IMPASS (verified)
  OR Undertaking "I" from applicant (if Pakistani citizenship was not formally renounced)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CANCELLATION - TYPE 4: POC DUE TO DIVORCE (FOR FOREIGN SPOUSE ONLY)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEGAL BASIS: As per Sub-Rule (5) of Rule (4) of NADRA (POC) Rules, 2002, a foreigner who is married to a citizen of Pakistan shall also be an eligible foreigner of Pakistan Origin, till the time he or she remains married to such citizens. As per policy, a Pakistani spouse must apply for the cancellation of their ex-spouse's POC (foreigner) after divorce.

DOCUMENT REQUIREMENT: Divorce papers

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: CANCELLATION - TYPE 5: DUP CANCELLATION]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DESCRIPTION: Citizens who have obtained two (02) or more than two (02) identity card(s) / CRC(s) with different Identity Numbers are flagged as "DUP" in the NADRA database. Such individuals have to process for cancellation of additional identity card(s) / CRC(s) and retain only one (01) identity card / CRC issued with correct particulars.
Upon application for DUP clearance, one of the card(s) / certificate(s) is retained while the other (duplicate) is surrendered automatically.
OVERSEAS: Submit application and supporting documents to NADRA through email ID: dup.op@nadra.gov.pk

DOCUMENT REQUIREMENT:
- Computerized Birth Registration Certificate
- If Birth Certificate is not issued: Other Government issued / Foreign documents reflecting particulars

FEE: Rs. 1,000/- (for same particulars) and Rs. 10,000/- (for different particulars)

[TOPIC: PAKID - GENERAL FAQs]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: What is NADRA PakID?
A: NADRA PakID is an online portal (mobile app) for filing applications for various types of Pakistani Identity cards. Through PakID, you can apply for:
- Smart National ID Card for Overseas Pakistanis (Smart NICOP): Fresh – Renewal – Modification – Reprint/Lost – Update of Non-Printable Field
- Smart ID: Renewal – Modification – Reprint lost – Update of Non-Printable Field
  NOTE: First time/fresh processing of Smart ID is NOT available at NADRA PakID.
- Smart Pakistan Origin Card (Smart POC): Fresh – Renewal – Modification
- Child Registration Certificate (CRC): Issuance of Fresh CRC for children up to one-year age

Q: Why should I choose NADRA PakID?
A:
- FAST: Apply hassle-free in few simple and easy steps.
- CONVENIENT: Apply for your identity card from anywhere in the world using the PakID mobile application (available on Android & iOS) from home or office.
- DELIVERY WORLDWIDE: Whether you live in Pakistan or abroad, your card will be delivered to your doorstep, whereas Certificates (CRC, FRC and Cancellation) will be sent to your PakID account inbox.

Q: Which services are NOT available online on Pak Identity (Mobile App)?
A: Passport.

Q: Can I apply for New Smart ID through Pak Identity?
A: No, this facility is not available at NADRA Pak Identity. You need to visit the nearest NADRA center to apply for new Smart ID.

Q: How does NADRA PakID system work?
A:
STEP 1 - REGISTRATION: Register your account on PakID Mobile Application (available on Android and iOS).
STEP 2 - CAPTURE/UPLOAD PHOTOGRAPH: Upload or Capture the photograph.
STEP 3 - PROVIDE REQUIRED DETAILS: Provide all required information in each respective section like Application details, Personal details, Relative details, Address details, Card delivery information.
STEP 4 - UPLOAD FINGERPRINTS & REQUIRED DOCUMENTS: Upload the required documents and fingerprints form. Fingerprints need to have a 600-dpi scanning quality or you can capture fingerprints through PakID mobile app in real time.
STEP 5 & 6 - MAKE PAYMENTS & SUBMIT APPLICATION:
- For applications related to Smart ID modification, Family Registration Certificate and Cancellation Certificate: first submit the application, then after approval by NADRA you will be notified through email to pay the fee.
- For all other applications: pay the fee before submitting the application.
NOTE: Payment can be made through Credit & Debit cards (MasterCard or Visa). Moreover, Smart ID and FRC application fees can also be paid through e-Sahulat franchise, Easypaisa and Jazz cash.

Q: I have registered my account with wrong email/mobile number. What should I do?
A: You can re-register your account with accurate email/mobile number. You have to provide new email ID.

Q: What should I do if I suspect that my password has been stolen or exposed to others?

Q: Can I create an account on the PakID if I haven't obtained or registered for a Pakistani citizen number?
A: No. Only your blood relatives (Father, Mother, Sister, Brother, Children above 18) or Spouse who are identity card (NIC, NICOP, POC) holders can create PakID account and process applications for themselves or other linked blood relatives.

Q: Is biometric verification mandatory for creation of PakID user account on Mobile app?
A: Yes, during account creation, after providing the citizen number details, the next step is biometric verification. Biometric verification is mandatory for activation of user account; unverified users cannot process any application.

Q: If I am not able to create my PakID account, Can I use my blood relatives or Spouse PakID account for applying online?
A: Yes, if due to any reason your PakID account could not be created/verified, you can apply through PakID account belonging to one of your spouse or blood relatives i.e. father, mother, sister, brother, spouse or children for processing of your identity document. Please make sure that the blood relative or spouse whose PakID account you are using has their NADRA identity card record already linked with your record.

Q: Can I process application through unverified PakID account?
A: Account verification is mandatory before processing of any application.

Q: I am creating user account on PakID mobile app, but I have not received the verification PIN/OTP in my email. What is the solution?
A: After providing the email address, you will receive a 4-digit verification PIN/OTP. If the email is not received in your inbox, try looking it in the spam/junk folder.

Q: Can I process application through PakID for individuals other than my blood relatives?
A: Applications can only be processed for blood relatives i.e. father, mother, sister, brother, spouse and children through PakID.

Q: I am verifying my account and my fingerprints are not verified through PakID mobile app, what should I do?
A: In case of fingerprint matching failure, you can verify your user account through facial matching.

Q: I am facing biometric verification issue for account verification on PakID mobile app. What should I do?
A: You will be given multiple attempts for biometric verification through fingerprints. If it doesn't match, you will be given the option to verify through facial. If this option doesn't work too, then you have to apply through a PakID account belonging to one of your blood relatives i.e. father, mother, sister, brother or children (above 18 years). Please make sure the blood relative's NADRA identity card record is already linked with you (Family tree). OR You can inform NADRA through NCCMS for refreshing the count of fingerprints verification attempts.

Q: I am facing location permission issue on the Mobile App. How to resolve this issue?
A: Turn on location services on your mobile before opening the PakID mobile app or allow the location permission option when PakID mobile app asks while logging in. Ensure your device has a stable Internet connection.

Q: I am facing this issue "Pak ID account not verified, Citizen Number already associated with another account". What should I do?
A: Applicant is allowed to create only one PakID account through his/her identity card number. The system does not allow creation of a 2nd account through the same identity card number. Therefore, login to the already created account using your ID card number. In case you have forgotten the password, click on the "Forgot password" button on the Mobile app login page and update/change the password and login.

Q: I am facing this issue "email already registered with another account". What should I do?
A: PakID mobile app users can use an email for just one account. If you are creating a new account, make sure the email address is not associated with any other PakID account.

Q: How to cancel an already created account on PakID Mobile app?
A: STEPS:
1. Log in to your account through PakID Mobile app.
2. Press the setting icons on the right top.
3. Select Profile Settings.
4. Select the option "Delete Account".
5. Check the box to accept terms and click on Yes.
6. Your account will be permanently deleted.
NOTE: After deleting the account, all the data in the account will be deleted as well and will not be recovered.

Q: What should I do if I face a technical issue while launching my application on PakID mobile app?
A: Contact NADRA through NADRA Centralized Complaint Management System (NCCMS). Provide complete information including Tracking ID, Citizen Number, relevant screenshots/documents.

Q: How to capture the fingerprints on PakID Mobile App?
A: Follow these steps for successful fingerprint capturing:
- Fingerprint tips must be aligned with the boxes.
- Use a plain background or a white paper while fingerprints capturing.
- Capture the fingerprints in well-lit environment.
- Keep the hand/fingers stationary by resting it on a fixed or solid surface.
- There must be appropriate gap between the fingers.
- While capturing the fingerprints, the hand should not be too far or too close to the camera.
- Don't tilt or curl the fingers.
NOTE: Applicant is allowed 10 attempts to capture fingerprints through the PakID mobile app. In case of failure, the applicant needs to provide the fingerprints on the verifier form.

Q: What is 600 dpi? How to scan the Fingerprints form at 600 dpi specification?
A: "DPI" stands for "dots per inch," a measurement of resolution in scanning. 600 DPI means 600 dots or pixels scanned per inch.
SCANNING STEPS:
1. Place the fingerprints form on the scanner.
2. Press the scan button on the scanner.
3. Click OK to proceed further.
4. Select the following parameters:
   - Select Grayscale from Color format drop down box.
   - Select JPG (JPG File) from File type drop down box.
   - Select 600 dpi in Resolution (DPI) box.
5. Click scan button.
6. Once scanning is completed, click Import button to save the scanned file at default printer location.
7. The file is now ready to be uploaded to PakID web application.

[TOPIC: DIGITAL ID - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DEFINITION: Digital Identity means a legal, secure, verifiable credentials issued to an eligible individual, enabling access to digital services, transactions, and interactions which shall be developed, issued and managed by NADRA under NADRA Ordinance, 2000. For legal entities, it shall be based on data maintained by the SECP or other relevant Federal or Provincial Government entities.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: DIGITAL ID - KEY ATTRIBUTES OF CITIZEN'S DIGITAL IDENTITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A citizen's Digital Identity is built on the following verified components:
- IDENTITY NUMBER: A valid CNIC, NICOP or POC with either fingerprint or facial biometric verification.
- MOBILE NUMBER: Must be verified via OTP (One-Time Password) for citizens residing in Pakistan. International mobile numbers are not verified, as they are not authenticated by PTA (Pakistan Telecommunication Authority).
- EMAIL ADDRESS: Must be verified through an OTP sent to the registered email.
- BIOMETRICS: Either fingerprints or facial recognition are required to verify identity biometrically.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: DIGITAL ID - CORE BUILDING BLOCKS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A Digital Identity is supported by the following foundational components:
- Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)
- Verifiable Credentials (VC)
- Dematerialized Card

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: DIGITAL ID - COMPLIANCE STANDARDS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PakID ensures strong security, privacy, and identity verification through compliance with the following internationally recognized standards:

AUTHENTICATION AND AUTHORIZATION STANDARDS:
- OAuth 2.0: Provides delegated access to APIs and apps, allowing users to authorize apps without sharing passwords.
- OpenID Connect (OIDC): Built on top of OAuth 2.0, it enables secure Single Sign-On (SSO) for users across multiple platforms.
- NIST 800-63: U.S. Digital Identity Guidelines defining Authentication Assurance Levels (AAL) and Identity Assurance Levels (IAL). PakID uses multi-factor authentication (MFA), risk-based authentication, and biometrics aligned with these levels.

SECURITY AND PRIVACY COMPLIANCE STANDARDS:
- TLS 1.2/1.3: Ensures secure data transmission via advanced encryption protocols.
- JWT (JSON Web Token): Allows secure and compact transmission of identity claims during authentication.
- Pakistan Security Standards: Implements pre-quantum cryptographic controls to comply with national security regulations.

COMPLIANCE AND IDENTITY GOVERNANCE FEATURES:
- GDPR/Pakistan Data Protection Bill: Enforces explicit user consent for data processing and sharing. Supports consent management and secure SSO under privacy regulations.

REAL-TIME MONITORING:
- ISO/IEC 27001: Implements a Security Management System with real-time monitoring, alerts, and controls to detect and prevent unauthorized access or fraud.

IDENTITY VERIFICATION STANDARDS:
- ISO/IEC 24760: Governs identity management, ensuring secure eKYC (Electronic Know Your Customer), biometric verification, and document validation.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: DIGITAL ID - REGISTRATION / ONBOARDING]

PLATFORM: PakID Mobile App (available on Google Play and Apple Store).

STEP 1: Create your account using your CNIC, email, and mobile number, and follow the guided registration steps in the app.

STEP 3: Once verified, you will receive your Digital ID, securely stored in your PakID Mobile App Vault.

[SECTION: SINGLE SIGN-ON (SSO)]
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: SSO - OVERVIEW]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
DESCRIPTION: PAKID SSO enables citizens to seamlessly access multiple government and private sector digital services using a single set of sign-in credentials. By leveraging the trust and security of the PakID platform, SSO eliminates the need for users to maintain multiple usernames and passwords across different services, enhancing user convenience and strengthening digital trust through centralized authentication and identity verification.

STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE: You may refer to the Step-by-step guide on the NADRA website for detailed instructions on how to use the Single Sign-On Feature.

HOW SSO DIFFERS FROM TRADITIONAL LOGINS: Traditional logins require separate accounts for each organization portal/service, while SSO provides one universal login across all connected government platforms.

IS SSO SECURE: Yes, SSO is verified through NADRA's official systems and uses multi-factor biometric verification for additional security.

PLATFORM SUPPORT: PakID SSO is designed to support integration with both web and mobile-based platforms. It utilizes OAuth 2.0 and OpenID Connect (OIDC). For mobile-based platforms, NADRA provides a specialized SDK available for both iOS and Android platforms.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: SSO - ELIGIBILITY]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA:
- Must possess a Foundational ID (NADRA-issued Citizen Identification Number).
- Must be able to verify identity through biometric verification as per NADRA policy.

VERIFICATION PARAMETERS:
- Mobile Number Verification through OTP (to establish possession of the mobile number).
- Biometric verification as per NADRA policy.
- Email Verification (verified through OTP).
- Identity Proofing: Fingerprint Verification (verified from NADRA citizen records).
- Facial verification (verified from NADRA citizen records).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: SSO - BENEFITS FOR CITIZENS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- ONE CREDENTIALS FOR ALL: Log in to multiple services using a single, secure Digital Identity; no need to remember different passwords. You only need to remember one login to access multiple government services — no more juggling different usernames and passwords.
- FASTER ACCESS TO SERVICES: Seamless and instant login to government and private sector platforms without repeated registrations.
- QUICK AND EASY ACCESS: Save time by logging in just once to use services like passport applications, tax systems, and health programs — all from one place.
- ENHANCED SECURITY AND PRIVACY: Reduce exposure to phishing and identity theft by avoiding weak or reused passwords across services.
- CONTROL AND TRANSPARENCY: Citizens can manage where and how their digital identity is being used through a centralized platform.
- INCLUSIVE ACCESS: Enables both local and overseas Pakistanis to securely interact with digital services from anywhere.
- CONSENT & AUDIT: Allows user-controlled "Profile" data sharing, detailed audit logs, and user access history.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: SSO - BENEFITS FOR ORGANIZATIONS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- VERIFIED USER BASE: Organizations can rely on pre-verified identities, reducing identity fraud and fake registrations.
- FASTER ONBOARDING: Eliminate lengthy registration forms and verification steps by leveraging NADRA's identity infrastructure.
- COST EFFECTIVE: Lowers the cost of building and maintaining separate identity and authentication systems. No need to verify the authenticated user again by sending OTP.
- IMPROVED USER EXPERIENCE: Frictionless access increases user satisfaction and engagement with digital platforms.
- REGULATORY COMPLIANCE: Ensures compliance with national digital identity and cybersecurity policies through standardized authentication.
- SECURITY & COMPLIANCE: PAKID SSO is OIDC / OAuth 2.0 compliant.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: SSO - DATA ATTRIBUTES PROVIDED TO ORGANIZATIONS]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Upon successful authentication, the following set of attributes is returned to the integrated organization:
- First Name
- Last Name
- Citizen Number
- Mobile Number
- Email Address

NOTE: PakID SSO is a standardized authentication service designed to verify users and share only essential, validated "profile data". Organizations that require access to additional citizen data fields or full identity verification should request NADRA's dedicated eKYC services, which are separate from the SSO offering and subject to additional user consent and authorization mechanisms.

IMPORTANT: PakID SSO is a standardized authentication product, NOT a data-sharing service. It is designed solely to verify that a user is a legitimate, authenticated and verified citizen of Pakistan.

Upon successful authentication, it only returns the following set of verified profile attributes:
- First Name
- Last Name
- Verified CNIC Number
- Verified Mobile Number
- Verified Email Address

SSO FEATURE NAME: "Sign in with PAKID" — available on any online platform (Web/Mobile) which is integrated with NADRA for this feature.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
[TOPIC: SSO - CITIZEN DATA ACCESS (MYINFO)]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Organizations that require access to verified citizen data fields must use the MyInfo product. PakID SSO is a mandatory prerequisite for MyInfo, as it provides the authentication framework and enables consent notifications to be sent to the citizen's registered and authorized device. Only after explicit consent is obtained from the citizen can the requested data fields be securely shared.

[TOPIC: CNIC FAQs - EXACT Q&A]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: How can I apply for a CNIC?
A: CNIC can be applied through two ways: (1) By visiting the nearest NADRA Center. (2) By using the Pak Identity Mobile App. At NRC: You will be issued a Qmatic token, photograph captured, fingerprints and signature taken, data entry done, Interview and approval by OIC, fee submission. App navigation: Go Pak ID Mobile App > ID Card > Myself / My blood Relative > Conversion.

Q: What is the "DUP" status?
A: Citizens who have obtained two (02) or more identity card(s)/CRC(s) with different Identity Numbers are flagged as "DUP" in the NADRA database. Such individuals have to process for cancellation of additional identity card(s)/CRC(s) and retain only one (01) identity card/CRC with correct particulars.

Q: How can I get myself cleared from "DUP"?
A: Visit any NADRA Registration Center (NRC) and apply for "DUP" clearance. Submit a notarized affidavit as per prescribed format. Fee: Rs. 1000/- (for same particulars) and Rs. 10,000/- (for different particulars). Upon submission, one card/certificate is retained while the other (duplicate) is surrendered automatically. Overseas Pakistanis: email dup.op@nadra.gov.pk

Q: I want to modify data on CNIC.
A: Please visit Registration Policy 6.0.1 in Downloads Section for documentary requirements against concerned Modification Type.

Q: I want to renew my identity card.
A: Scanned image of ID card (NICOP/CNIC) is required for renewal. Only Picture will be updated on the card. Note: If you wish to modify any other field at the time of renewal, please apply in modification category.

Q: I want to modify data on my CNIC (Non-printable Field).
A: Please visit Registration Policy 6.0.1 in Downloads Section for documentary requirements in non-printable fields.

Q: My Identity Card (CNIC) is stolen / lost.
A: No document is required; card will be issued with existing data.

Q: I want to cancel deceased family member identity card (SCNIC/CNIC).
A: Please visit Registration Policy 6.0.1 in Downloads Section for documentary requirements in non-printable fields.

Q: I want to expedite my applied Application.
A: You can expedite from Normal/Urgent to Executive by paying the remaining fee. Steps: (1) Login to Pak Identity Mobile App. (2) Go to "NADRA Miscellaneous Services" and select "Application Priority". (3) Insert the Tracking ID and Pin Code and press "Next". (4) Pay the difference of amount. (5) You will receive an acknowledgement by NADRA.

Q: I want to surrender my identity card (CNIC).
A: If an individual has renounced Pakistani nationality and obtained nationality of any single national country (with which Pakistan does not have dual national arrangements), he/she shall apply to surrender the ID card by providing valid passport of that country.

Q: What is a Family Registration Certificate (FRC)?
A: The FRC is an official document that verifies the family composition as recorded in NADRA's database as well as un-registered members with deceased/alive status. It is primarily used for embassy-related matters, various legal/official purposes and serves as a legal document.

Q: What are the categories of FRC available?
A: FRC can be applied under four categories: (1) By Birth: Includes details of parents and siblings. (2) By Marriage: Includes details of spouse and children. (3) By Adoption: Includes details of guardian and adopted family members. (4) By All: Includes details of both alpha and beta family members.

Q: How can I apply for Family Registration Certificate?
A: You can apply through: (1) NADRA Registration Center (NRC): Obtain a token, complete biometric verification, submit the fee and receive FRC copy immediately. (2) Pak Identity Mobile App: Apply online and receive a digital FRC copy within 24 hours.

Q: How many days my FRC application will take?
A: Through NRC: delivered in real time. Through Pak Identity Mobile App: PDF version available within 24 hours. Note: Processing time starts after submission of fee.

Q: How Can I apply for CRC from NADRA Center?
A: Steps: (1) Obtain a token. (2) Biometric Verification. (3) Photographs and/or fingerprints of children is mandatory. (4) Your data will be entered and reviewed. (5) Attestation by Spouse/Guardian/Gazetted officer. (6) Fee submission: (Executive Rs 500 – 01 days) & (Normal Rs 50 – 07 days). (7) CRC will be printed and handed over upon completion of the processing period. Biometric Verification/Attestation for fresh cases: In case any Blood relative (Father, Mother, Brother and Sister) or spouse is available, his/her biometrics will be captured. Otherwise, form will be printed and given to you for attestation. Form shall be submitted to the concerned NRC after attestation.

Q: How Can I apply for CRC from Pak ID Mobile App?
A: Steps: (1) Log in to your Pak ID account or create a new account. (2) On "Home" page, go to "Family" and select "CRC or B-form". (3) Choose "Myself" or select "My Blood Relatives Category". (4) Select process category: (Executive Rs 500 – 01 days) & (Normal Rs 50 – 07 days). (5) "Start Application", review prefilled information and proceed. (6) Verify parent's details. (7) Enter Disability details (if required). (8) Capture or upload the child's photograph and fingerprints (where required). (9) Verify the fingerprints of one parent. (10) Upload the required documents. (11) Review and submit the application. (12) Fee submission. (13) CRC will be downloaded (PDF) and appeared in ID Vault upon completion of the processing period.

Q: Which documents are required for processing of fresh CRC?
A: CRC can be obtained by providing documented proof of birth i.e. Computerized Birth Registration Certificate issued by the Union Council/Municipal Committee/Cantonment (for inland births) and Detailed Birth Certificate or S-1 Form (for abroad births).

Q: Am I eligible for POC?
A: You are eligible for POC if: (1) You were a Pakistani national and have surrendered the nationality. (2) You are a foreign national, married to a Pakistani citizen and your parents are of Pakistani origin or ever held Pakistani nationality. (3) You are a foreign national and your parents or grandparents are or were Pakistani nationals. (4) You are a foreign national and any of your relatives (brother, sister, uncle, aunt) are or were Pakistani nationals. NOT eligible (foreign spouse): Citizens/nationals of India, Israel and Taiwan; citizens of countries not recognized by Pakistan; citizens of any enemy country.

Q: How do I surrender/cancel my POC?
A: You can apply for cancellation of Pakistan Origin Card (POC) through Pak-Identity Mobile App.

Q: How can I apply for POC?
A: You can apply for your Pakistan Origin Card (POC) by visiting Pak Identity Mobile App and have it delivered to your doorstep. Categories: New (applying for the first time), Modification (change in existing POC data), Renewal (applying in case of expired POC), Cancellation (apply if you wish to surrender your POC).

(7) Fee submission: (Executive Rs 2500) & (Urgent Rs 1500) & (Normal Rs 750) excluding delivery fee. (8) Juvenile Card will be printed and handed over upon completion of the processing period.

Q: How long will it take from application completion to delivery (Juvenile Card)?
A: Executive: 09 days. Urgent: 23 days. Normal: 31 days. Note: Processing time starts after submission of fee.

Q: Which documents are required for processing of Juvenile Card?
A: Juvenile Card can be obtained by providing documentary proof of birth i.e. Computerized Birth Registration Certificate issued by the Union Council/Municipal Committee/Cantonment (for inland births) and Detailed Birth Certificate or S-1 Form (for abroad births).

[TOPIC: DIGITAL ID FAQs - EXACT Q&A]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q: What is Digital ID?
A: Digital Identity means a legal, secure, verifiable credentials issued to an eligible individual, enabling access to digital services, transactions, and interactions which shall be developed, issued and managed by NADRA under NADRA Ordinance, 2000. For legal entities, it shall be based on data maintained by the SECP or other relevant Federal or Provincial Government entities.

Q: What are the key attributes that make up a citizen's Digital Identity?
A: A citizen's Digital Identity is built on: (1) Identity Number: A valid CNIC, NICOP or POC with either fingerprint or facial biometric verification. (2) Mobile Number: Must be verified via OTP for citizens residing in Pakistan. International mobile numbers are not verified, as they are not authenticated by PTA (Pakistan Telecommunication Authority). (3) Email Address: Must be verified through an OTP sent to the registered email. (4) Biometrics: Either fingerprints or facial recognition are required to verify identity biometrically.

Q: What are the core building blocks of a Digital Identity?
A: A Digital Identity is supported by: Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), Verifiable Credentials (VC), Dematerialized Card.

Q: What standards does PakID comply with for digital identity and data security?
A: Authentication and Authorization Standards: OAuth 2.0, OpenID Connect (OIDC), NIST 800-63 (AAL and IAL, MFA, risk-based authentication, biometrics). Security and Privacy Compliance Standards: TLS 1.2/1.3, JWT (JSON Web Token), Pakistan Security Standards (pre-quantum cryptographic controls). Compliance and Identity Governance Features: GDPR/Pakistan Data Protection Bill. Real-Time Monitoring: ISO/IEC 27001. Identity Verification Standards: ISO/IEC 24760 (eKYC, biometric verification, document validation).

Q: How can I register and complete the onboarding for a Digital ID?
A: Register through the PakID Mobile App (Google Play and Apple Store). Step 1: Create your account using your CNIC, email, and mobile number. Step 2: Verify your identity using biometric data like your photo and fingerprints, securely matched with NADRA's records. Step 3: Once verified, you will receive your Digital ID, securely stored in your PakID Mobile App Vault. Step 4: Start using your Digital ID to quickly access government, health, education, and financial services.

Q: What are the requirements to register for a Digital ID?
A: You must be at least 18 years old. A SIM issued on your CNIC. Biometric verification using your photo and fingerprints.

Q: What are the benefits of having a Digital ID?
A: Secure login to multiple government services with Single Sign-On. Quick document sharing through the PakID Vault. Seamless access to healthcare, banking, education, and passport systems. Control over data sharing through consent-based systems. Offline verification with QR codes.

Q: What is Single Sign-On (SSO)?
A: PAKID SSO enables citizens to seamlessly access multiple government and private sector digital services using a single set of sign-in credentials. It eliminates the need for users to maintain multiple usernames and passwords across different services, enhancing user convenience and strengthening digital trust through centralized authentication and identity verification.

Q: How is SSO different from traditional logins?
A: Traditional logins require separate accounts for each organization portal/service, while SSO provides one universal login across all connected government platforms.

Q: Is SSO secure?
A: Yes, SSO is verified through NADRA's official systems and uses multi-factor biometric verification for additional security.

Q: Which users are eligible to use PAKID Single Sign-on Feature?
A: User must first onboard onto the PAK Identity platform. Eligibility Criteria: Must possess a Foundational ID (NADRA-issued Citizen Identification Number) and be able to verify identity through biometric verification as per NADRA policy. Verification Parameters: Mobile Number Verification through OTP, Biometric verification, Email Verification (OTP), Fingerprint Verification (from NADRA citizen records), Facial verification (from NADRA citizen records). Citizen who is successfully onboarded will be eligible to use "Sign in with PAKID" feature on any integrated online platform.

Q: What are the benefits of PakID Single Sign-On (SSO) for both citizens and organizations?
A: For Citizens: One Credentials for All (no more juggling different usernames and passwords), Faster Access to Services, Enhanced Security and Privacy, Control and Transparency, Inclusive Access, Consent & Audit. For Organizations: Verified User Base, Faster Onboarding, Cost Effective (no separate authentication systems, no OTP costs), Improved User Experience, Regulatory Compliance, Security & Compliance (OIDC/OAuth 2.0 compliant).

Q: Which parameters are provided by PAKID Single Sign-On to organizations?
A: PakID SSO is a standardized authentication service. Upon successful authentication, the following set of attributes is returned: First Name, Last Name, Citizen Number, Mobile Number, Email Address. Organizations requiring additional citizen data fields should request NADRA's dedicated eKYC services.

Q: Will citizen data be provided by PakID Single Sign-On (SSO) to organizations?
A: No, PakID SSO is a standardized authentication product, not a data-sharing service. It is designed solely to verify that a user is a legitimate, authenticated and verified citizen of Pakistan. Upon successful authentication, it only returns: First Name, Last Name, Verified CNIC Number, Verified Mobile Number, Verified Email Address.

Q: How can an organization access citizen data fields through the Digital Identity ecosystem?
A: Organizations must use the MyInfo product. PakID SSO is a mandatory prerequisite for MyInfo. Only after explicit consent is obtained from the citizen can the requested data fields be securely shared. Integration with NADRA's PakID SSO must be enforced as a foundational requirement.

Q: Is PAKID SSO available for integration with both Web-Based and mobile platforms?
A: Yes, PakID SSO supports integration with both web and mobile-based platforms. It utilizes OAuth 2.0 and OpenID Connect (OIDC). For mobile-based platforms, NADRA provides a specialized SDK available for both iOS and Android platforms.

Q: What is the PakID Vault?
A: PakID Vault is a secure storage space where you can save, manage, and share your important documents and certificates digitally.

Q: What types of documents can I store in the PakID Vault?
A: You can store CNIC, academic degrees, licenses, training certificates, and other verifiable documents.

Q: How can I share documents from the PakID Vault?
A: Documents stored in the PakID Vault can be shared directly or by using QR code.

Q: Who can view my documents in the PakID Vault?
A: Your documents in the PakID Vault are completely private and can only be viewed by others when you choose to share them. No one can access or open your documents without your explicit consent.
